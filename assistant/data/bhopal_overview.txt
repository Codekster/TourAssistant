Bhopal - The City of Lakes

Bhopal, the capital city of Madhya Pradesh, is known as the "City of Lakes" due to its numerous natural and artificial lakes. The city beautifully blends historical heritage with modern development.

Major Attractions:

Upper Lake (Bhojtal):
- One of the largest artificial lakes in Asia
- Perfect for boating and water sports
- Beautiful sunset views from the lakeside
- Boat Club offers various water activities
- Best time to visit: October to March

Taj-ul-Masajid:
- One of the largest mosques in India
- Built during the reign of Nawab Sultan Jahan Begum
- Beautiful Indo-Islamic architecture
- Open to visitors except during prayer times
- Free entry for tourists

Bharat Bhavan:
- Multi-arts complex and museum
- Showcases contemporary Indian art
- Regular cultural performances and exhibitions
- Houses tribal and folk art collections
- Entry fee: Rs. 20 for adults

Lower Lake:
- Connected to Upper Lake
- Smaller but equally beautiful
- Popular for morning walks
- Good fishing spot
- Surrounded by parks and gardens

Van Vihar National Park:
- Urban wildlife sanctuary
- Home to tigers, lions, leopards
- Open safari experience
- Entry fee: Rs. 30 for adults
- Open: 6:30 AM to 6:00 PM

Local Cuisine:
- Bhopali Gosht Korma
- Seekh Kebabs
- Bhopali Paan
- Jalebi and Poha for breakfast
- Best food areas: Chowk Bazaar, New Market

Transportation:
- Well connected by road, rail, and air
- Local buses and auto-rickshaws available
- Raja Bhoj Airport for domestic flights
- Bhopal Junction is the main railway station
